Here are some practical options, roughly from safest/simplest to more complex:

## Immediate Easy Wins (Do These First)

**High-Yield Savings Account (HYSA)**
- Regular banks often pay 0.01% interest
- HYSAs currently pay around 4-5%
- That's potentially $2,000-2,500/year doing almost nothing different
- Look at: Marcus, Ally, SoFi, Capital One 360
- FDIC insured, same safety as regular bank

**Money Market Accounts** - similar to HYSAs, worth comparing rates

---

## Low Risk Options

- **CDs (Certificates of Deposit)** - lock money for fixed period, slightly higher rates
- **Treasury Bills** - government backed, competitive rates, very safe
- **I-Bonds** - inflation-protected government bonds

---

## Longer Term Investing

- **Index funds** - broad market investments, low fees, historically solid returns
- **Roth IRA or Traditional IRA** - tax-advantaged retirement accounts
- **401k** - especially if your employer matches contributions

---

## Practical Suggestions

- Keep 3-6 months expenses as emergency fund (liquid)
- The HYSA alone is a genuinely good immediate move
- Read **"The Simple Path to Wealth"** by JL Collins - genuinely beginner friendly
- Consider a **fee-only financial advisor** for personalized guidance
