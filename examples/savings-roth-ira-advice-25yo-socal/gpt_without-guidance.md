# 3. Credit cards: one card vs multiple cards
## Short answer
You do **not** need to consolidate to one card, but you should simplify.

The real goal is:
- use cards for rewards and credit building
- never carry a balance
- automate full monthly payment
- avoid annual fees unless the rewards clearly beat the fee

## Good rule
Use **1–2 main cards** for most spending, and keep older no-annual-fee cards open.

That gives you:
- easy budgeting
- strong credit utilization
- preserved average age of accounts
- better total available credit

# 5. Should you cancel old credit cards?
Usually: **no**, especially if they have **no annual fee**.

Keep them open because they help:
- average age of accounts
- total available credit
- utilization ratio

## Cancel only if:
- there’s an annual fee you don’t want to pay
- the issuer is annoying or restrictive
- the card tempts overspending
- you’re simplifying an account that no longer makes sense

## Important
If you keep old cards:
- use them for a tiny recurring charge every few months
- set autopay
- prevent issuer closure due to inactivity

So for cards like Discover It or a no-fee US Bank card, I’d generally **keep them open**.
