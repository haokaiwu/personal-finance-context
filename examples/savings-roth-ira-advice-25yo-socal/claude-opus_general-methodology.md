## Credit Card Strategy

**Don't cancel your older cards.** Your credit age is one of the easier ways to keep your scores high, and closing old accounts shortens your average age of credit and reduces total available credit (which raises your utilization ratio). Just put a small recurring charge on each one — a subscription, gas, whatever — and auto-pay the full statement balance. Zero effort, protects your score.

**Yes, consolidate your main spending onto one strong rewards card.** At ~$3k/month in expenses, you're leaving real money on the table with basic cards. The **Chase Sapphire Preferred** is a solid pick for you:

- 3x on dining and streaming, 2x on travel, and the points transfer to airline/hotel partners at strong value
- The $95 annual fee pays for itself quickly at your spend level
- The sign-up bonus alone (usually 60,000+ points) is worth $750+ in travel

One thing to watch: Chase has the **5/24 rule** — they'll deny you if you've opened 5+ cards in the last 24 months. With only a couple cards, you should be fine.

**The play:** Use the Sapphire as your daily driver for everything, pay the full statement balance every month (never carry a balance), and keep the Discover and US Bank open with small auto-charged subscriptions.

### 2. Then start the Roth IRA
Once the emergency fund is set, **open a Roth IRA immediately and start maxing it out.** Here's why this is a no-brainer for you at 25:

After funding the Roth, you'll still have ~$1,750/month left over. That opens up your next set of decisions (taxable investing, saving for a house, your wife's Roth IRA, etc.) — but get these two locked in first.
