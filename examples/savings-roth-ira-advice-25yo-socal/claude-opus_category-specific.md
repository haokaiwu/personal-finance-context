## 1. Credit Card Strategy: Consolidate, Upgrade, or Cancel?

**Grab the Chase Sapphire Preferred (or a similar travel/rewards card) and make it your primary card.** With 780+ scores and disciplined pay-in-full habits, you're the exact person rewards cards are designed for. The Discover It Miles and US Bank cards are fine starter cards, but they leave money on the table compared to a card with bonus categories and a strong points ecosystem.

Here's the game plan:
- **Use one primary rewards card** for the bulk of your spending. Chase Sapphire Preferred is a solid choice — it earns well on dining and travel, has a useful sign-up bonus, and the points are flexible. If you don't travel much, look at a flat 2% cashback card like the Citi Double Cash instead.
- **Do NOT cancel the old cards.** Closing them shortens your average credit age and reduces your total available credit — both of which would ding your score. Just sock-drawer them. Put a small recurring charge on each (like a streaming subscription) and set them to autopay so they stay active.
- **You don't need to split spending across cards** for credit score purposes. Using one card and paying it off monthly is simpler and still keeps your utilization low.
